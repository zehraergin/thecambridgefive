Events of the Cambridge Five

  • 1930s – Recruitment at Cambridge
    During the 1930s, the future members of the Cambridge Five were recruited by Soviet intelligence while studying at Cambridge University. Many were attracted to communist ideas because of the Great Depression and the rise of fascism in Europe. They were instructed to hide their political beliefs and pursue influential government careers.
  • 1940s – Infiltration of the British Government
    During World War II and the years immediately after, the Cambridge Five gained positions in British intelligence and government agencies. They passed thousands of classified documents to the Soviet Union. This period was when they caused the greatest damage to British and Western security.
  • 1951 – Burgess and Maclean Defect
    British intelligence began investigating Donald Maclean after suspecting him of espionage. Guy Burgess warned him, and both men fled to the Soviet Union. Their sudden disappearance confirmed that Soviet spies had penetrated the British government.
  • 1963 – Philby Defects
    After years of suspicion, Kim Philby escaped to Moscow in 1963. His defection confirmed that he had been spying for the Soviets for decades. The event became one of the biggest intelligence scandals of the Cold War.
  • 1979 – Anthony Blunt Exposed
    Although Anthony Blunt had secretly confessed years earlier, his role as a Soviet spy was publicly revealed in 1979. The revelation shocked Britain and renewed interest in the Cambridge Five case. It also led to criticism of British intelligence and security screening practices.
