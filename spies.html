Meet The Cambridge Five

Kim Philby

Kim Philby was the most influential member of the Cambridge Five and worked for MI6 while secretly spying for the Soviet Union. He passed highly classified intelligence to Moscow for decades before defecting to the Soviet Union in 1963.

Guy Burgess

Guy Burgess worked in British intelligence and the Foreign Office while secretly providing information to the Soviets. In 1951, he fled to Moscow with Donald Maclean, helping expose the existence of the Cambridge spy ring.

Donald Maclean

Donald Maclean was a British diplomat who gave the Soviet Union valuable information about British and American foreign policy. When British authorities became suspicious, he escaped to Moscow with Burgess in 1951.

Anthony Blunt

Anthony Blunt worked for MI5 during World War II while secretly spying for the Soviet Union. After the war, he became a famous art historian, and his role as a spy was publicly revealed in 1979.

John Cairncross

John Cairncross worked in several British government departments and passed important military and intelligence information to the Soviets during World War II. Although less famous than the others, historians believe he provided some of the most valuable intelligence of the group.
